I'm a PhD candidate in **Earth and Space Science** at the **Lassonde School of Engineering, York University**, supervised by **Dr. Regina Lee**. My research sits between optical instrumentation and image processing, applied to **Resident Space Object (RSO) detection, tracking, and characterization** — built from ground-based telescopes, stratospheric balloon payloads, and on-orbit star-tracker imagery.

My doctoral work extends my MSc thesis, *Image Processing for Stratospheric-Based Space Situational Awareness*, toward space-based and high-altitude platforms for tracking debris and active satellites in increasingly congested orbital regimes. I've contributed to the **RSONAR** stratospheric balloon program and the **SOBER** mission, selected through ESA's REXUS/BEXUS programme — from payload design and sensor integration through to the detection algorithms that process the resulting imagery.

Alongside research, I work as a teaching assistant across astronomy, planetary science, optics, and geophysics courses, and take part regularly in public space-science outreach.
