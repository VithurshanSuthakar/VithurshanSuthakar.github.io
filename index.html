Imaging and tracking objects in Earth orbit, from telescopes on the ground to payloads in the stratosphere.

I'm a PhD candidate in Earth and Space Science at York University, supervised by Dr. Regina Lee. My work sits between optical instrumentation and image processing, applied to Resident Space Object (RSO) detection, tracking, and characterization — built from ground-based telescopes, stratospheric balloon payloads, and on-orbit star-tracker imagery.

My doctoral research extends my MSc thesis, Image Processing for Stratospheric-Based Space Situational Awareness, toward space-based and high-altitude platforms for tracking debris and active satellites in increasingly congested orbital regimes. I've contributed to the RSONAR stratospheric balloon program and the SSA Stratospheric mission, selected through ESA's REXUS/BEXUS programme — from payload design and sensor integration through to the detection algorithms that process the resulting imagery.

Alongside research, I work as a teaching assistant across astronomy, planetary science, optics, and geophysics courses, and take part regularly in public space-science outreach.
